Light Housekeeping: What to Expect

At Alice Care, we want to provide clarity on what our caregivers can and cannot do when it comes to housekeeping tasks. Our services include tidy-up support that keeps your immediate living spaces safe and clean. This differs from full housekeeping or deep cleaning services, which fall outside the caregiver role. Our caregivers follow these guidelines to deliver consistent, safe, and effective non-medical home care within the scope of their responsibilities.

Understanding the Difference

Tidy-Up Services (Included)

Basic household support that maintains a safe, clean, and orderly environment for the client. Tasks connect directly to the care plan and focus on the client's immediate living areas, personal items, and daily comfort.

Housekeeping Services (Not Included)

Deep cleaning, heavy labor, repairs, full-household cleaning, or tasks that extend beyond the client's personal care needs. These services require a dedicated housekeeping or cleaning professional.

Caregiver Do's & Don'ts

The following guidelines define the tasks our caregivers will and will not perform during their visits.

Caregivers Will Caregivers Will Not
Keep the client's immediate living areas safe, tidy, and organized. Provide deep cleaning or maid service.
Wash dishes used during the shift or after meal preparation. Scrub entire kitchens, clean ovens, or perform heavy appliance cleaning unless specifically approved as basic cleanup.
Wipe counters, tables, and surfaces used during caregiving activities. Use strong chemicals or hazardous cleaning products without office or family instruction.
Sweep or vacuum light traffic areas used by the client for safety and cleanliness. Move heavy furniture or large objects.
Mop small spills and lightly clean floors when needed for safety. Wax floors, shampoo carpets, or perform restorative floor care.
Make the bed and straighten the client's room. Rearrange the household or organize rooms unrelated to the client's care.
Change bed linens when needed. Handle excessive household laundry for the whole family.
Do the client's personal laundry, towels, and bedding as assigned. Wash laundry for guests, relatives, or other household members unless authorized.
Take out kitchen trash and regular household trash used during the shift. Handle hazardous waste, biohazard cleanup, or unsafe materials.
Put away groceries and keep food storage areas orderly. Perform major pantry cleanouts or non-care-related household projects.
Throw away spoiled food when instructed and keep commonly used areas sanitary. Discard valuables, personal papers, or belongings without permission.
Tidy clutter that creates fall risk in areas used by the client. Clean rooms or spaces not connected to the client's care needs unless assigned.
Clean the bathroom lightly after assisting the client, such as wiping surfaces and keeping it safe. Perform intensive bathroom scrubbing, mold removal, or maintenance work.
Report unsafe home conditions, broken equipment, infestations, or sanitation concerns to the office or family. Perform repairs, maintenance, pest control, or unsafe cleanup.
Follow the care plan and complete only the housekeeping tasks connected to non-medical home care. Perform tasks outside the care plan or outside the non-medical scope.

Caregiver Scope of Role

Alice Care caregivers follow strict guidelines and work only within the limits of their assigned role. They will not exceed the scope of non-medical home care services as defined by the care plan and CDSS regulations. If you need housekeeping beyond what our caregivers provide, we recommend contacting a professional cleaning service.
Regulatory Disclosure: Alice Care operates under CDSS License #344700080 in compliance with the Home Care Services Consumer Protection Act (AB 1217). Light housekeeping tasks fall within the non-medical services authorized for Registered Home Care Aides. For more information, view the CDSS Home Care Services Fact Sheet.
