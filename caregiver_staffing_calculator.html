Determine the Right Staffing for Each Client

Answer the questions below to receive a staffing recommendation grounded in our published safety policy. The calculator runs every rule in real time, lists the triggers that drive the decision, and notes the equipment the shift requires. Use the print button to attach the result to the care plan.

Single caregiver is the standard model. Most Alice Care shifts run with one trained caregiver. The calculator escalates to two caregivers only when weight, mobility, bed-bound status, or a specific bed-bound trigger crosses the policy threshold.

3Active Assist Available?

This question resolves whether Alice Care sends one or two caregivers when the policy thresholds would otherwise require two. A responsible family member can fill the second-caregiver role if present and capable for every shift.

4Bed-Bound Status

Mark the client bed-bound when they remain in bed most or all of the day, require help for every transfer, cannot reposition independently, or use a hospital bed.

5Support Equipment Available on Site

Equipment availability changes staffing. Per VA Safe Patient Handling Algorithms and NIOSH guidance, the right device can transform a two-caregiver task into a safe one-caregiver task. Select every device confirmed available, in working order, and matched to the client weight capacity.

Lead Caregiver Lift Certification

A lead caregiver who holds current Hoyer or sit-to-stand competency on file, plus documented field experience with the client, can safely operate the lift solo for cooperative non-weight-bearing clients. This certification override allows Alice Care to send one caregiver instead of two.

6Specific Care Tasks Required

Select every task the shift involves. Tasks marked "bed-bound trigger" only force two caregivers when combined with bed-bound status. Single-caregiver shifts remain the norm for ambulatory clients with active assist.
