Care for Other Household Members

At Alice Care, our caregivers provide authorized, non-medical home care services for your identified client only. California law defines home care services as personal assistance provided to a specific client by a Registered Home Care Aide. While our caregivers are compassionate and attentive, they must keep their time, focus, and duties centered on the person named in the care plan. This email explains what occasional, incidental help may look like versus when a separate authorization becomes required.

Understanding the Boundary

Incidental Help (Permitted)

Brief, one-time, low-risk assistance that does not delay or reduce care for the primary client. It must be minor, occasional, and unplanned — not part of the regular shift routine or care plan.

Separate Care (Not Permitted)

Recurring, expected, personal, or time-consuming support for another household member. Once assistance becomes routine or involves personal care, supervision, or recovery support, it requires separate intake and authorization.

Caregiver Do's & Don'ts

These guidelines define what our caregivers will and will not do when another household member is present during a scheduled visit.

Caregivers Will Not
Keep all attention and care focused on the authorized client throughout the visit.
Provide regular or expected assistance to any person not named in the care plan.
Hand a household member a meal already being prepared for the client during the visit.
Prepare separate, recurring meals for a spouse, family member, or household resident.
Offer one-time, low-risk assistance such as bringing water or a light item to another household member.
Assist another household member with bathing, dressing, toileting, transfers, or mobility on any basis.
Help a household member stand from a chair once during an isolated moment if the client's care is fully unaffected.
Divide attention between the authorized client and another household member during the shift.
Notify the Alice Care office immediately if another household member's needs appear to be increasing or ongoing.
Provide recovery support after a hospitalization for any household member not on the care plan.
Follow the authorized care plan and limit all duties to the client's approved, non-medical care needs.
Supervise or monitor another household member while the primary client still requires attention.
Communicate any household changes to the office so Alice Care can assess whether separate services are needed.
Perform any medical or skilled task for any household member, authorized client or otherwise.
Escalate immediately if a household member's condition or needs change in a way that affects shift priorities.
Allow support for another household member to become a planned or expected part of the shift routine.

When Separate Services Are Required

If another household member begins to need ongoing personal care, supervision, mobility assistance, transfers, toileting, routine meals, or recovery support after a hospitalization, the caregiver must notify the Alice Care office immediately. Alice Care will determine whether separate intake, a separate care plan, or a second caregiver is required. The caregiver must stop any informal expansion of duties before a formal review takes place.

Regulatory Disclosure: Alice Care operates under CDSS License #344700080 in compliance with the Home Care Services Consumer Protection Act (AB 1217). California's Domestic Worker Bill of Rights also specifies that a personal attendant loses that classification if more than 20% of work time shifts to duties unrelated to supervising, feeding, or dressing the person in care.
