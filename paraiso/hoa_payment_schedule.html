At current fee levels, HOA maintenance fees cover only 80.8% of 2025 operating expenses — a gap of approximately $1.75M MXN (~USD $100,344). This shortfall has widened each year as operating costs grew 9.1% since 2023 while fee revenue remained flat. The HOA must fund the remaining balance through supplemental assessments, reserves, or capital contributions. The 2025 expense report already shows a new unbudgeted “Capital Contribution Recovery” line of $75,978 as evidence of this pressure.

Governance note: Under Mexican condominium law (Ley de Condominios), voting rights at owner assemblies are allocated by millesimis. With 26.4% millesimis, the Builder holds significant weight on resolutions requiring a qualified majority (typically 75%+). Private owners collectively control 73.6% — sufficient for ordinary resolutions but not a supermajority alone. Any vote on fee increases, budget approval, or Capital Contribution Recovery terms requires understanding this distribution.
