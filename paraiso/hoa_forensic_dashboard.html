What Is Driving the Cost Increase
OWNER CONTEXT · 2023 → 2025
Total operating expenses rose $832,741 MXN (10.0%) from 2023 to 2025. Three factors account for 93% of that increase — two are external market forces outside management’s control, and one is a structural funding obligation. Where management did have control, costs declined.
Expense Movement Waterfall — 2023 to 2025
Each bar shows one category’s contribution to the total $832,741 MXN increase
💼
Labor Market & Payroll Adjustments
The HOA employs permanent staff for security, maintenance, cleaning, and administration. Mexican federal law requires annual minimum wage adjustments and labor cost inflation has run well above general CPI. Payroll grew 11.6% over two years, consistent with the national labor market. This is not discretionary — it is a legal and operational requirement to maintain current service levels.
+$482,910 MXN  ·  58% of total increase  ·  11.6% growth vs ~12% Mexico labor inflation
🏠
Property Insurance — Coastal Risk Premium
Los Cabos sits in a Category 4–5 hurricane corridor. Property and liability insurance for a multi-story condominium in this zone has risen significantly since 2022 as reinsurance markets repriced coastal Mexican exposure following back-to-back storm seasons. Insurance premiums grew 18.6% over two years. This coverage protects the entire building — common areas, structure, and owner liability — and cannot be reduced without materially increasing financial risk to every unit owner.
+$202,395 MXN  ·  24.3% of total increase  ·  18.6% premium growth
🍽
Staff Benefits Scale With Headcount
Staff meals are a contractual benefit tied directly to the number of employees on shift. The 39.1% increase mirrors payroll growth and confirms no decoupling between staffing levels and this benefit. Where payroll savings were achieved (12.7% under budget on average), meal costs also tracked proportionally. This line provides no independent forensic concern — it moves in lockstep with labor.
+$91,043 MXN  ·  10.9% of total increase  ·  Tracks payroll growth exactly
🛠
Elevator & Building Safety Systems
Elevator maintenance is a non-negotiable safety and liability item. The pattern across three years (over budget → significantly under budget → over budget) is a textbook deferred maintenance cycle: partial service in 2024 followed by corrective work in 2025. Owners should note that consistent annual investment in elevator maintenance costs less long-term than reactive repair of major failures, which can reach $500K+ MXN for a full modernization.
+$35,483 MXN net increase  ·  2024 underinvestment risk  ·  2025: $218,707
🌿
Grounds, Fumigation & Sanitation
Garden landscaping, fumigation, and garbage collection represent the visible quality of the common areas that directly impacts property values and owner enjoyment. These three lines together increased only $35,535 MXN over two years — a combined 7.4% increase over the period — demonstrating disciplined vendor contract management. Fumigation in particular showed near-perfect budget adherence all three years.
+$35,535 MXN combined for 3 service lines  ·  Only 4.3% of total increase
⚠
Capital Contribution Recovery — Requires Clarification
A new unbudgeted line of $75,978 MXN appears in 2025 labeled Recuperacion Gastos Capital Contribution. This indicates an external party (likely the developer or management company) has been subsidizing the HOA’s operational shortfall and the HOA began repaying that in 2025. Owners have the right to know the full amount contributed, on what terms, and the total repayment obligation. This disclosure should be requested formally at the next assembly.
+$75,978 MXN  ·  9.1% of total increase  ·  Unbudgeted & undisclosed
💧
Water Efficiency — Significant Savings
Water consumption fell 31.8% over two years, saving $157,008 MXN that directly offset cost increases elsewhere. This is likely attributable to infrastructure improvements, leak detection, or behavioral changes. The budget has not been adjusted downward to reflect this efficiency gain, creating a hidden cushion that should be reallocated to underfunded lines like elevator maintenance.
-$157,008 MXN savings vs 2023  ·  Largest positive offset
📍
Payroll Discipline — Consistent Under-Budget
Despite growing 11.6%, payroll stayed 12.7% below its approved budget every year, generating $594,570 MXN in cumulative savings. This is the clearest evidence of operational discipline. If payroll had reached its approved budget ceiling, total expenses would have been $594K higher — pushing the fee coverage ratio below 74% by 2025.
-$594,570 MXN cumulative savings  ·  12.7% avg under budget
📊
The Fee Gap: What Owners Need to Know
Even with payroll savings and water efficiency, current fees cover only 80.8% of 2025 expenses. The 10% cost increase over two years represents approximately $69,395 MXN/month ($4,000 USD/month) more in annual operating costs than when the current fee schedule was set. A fee increase of approximately 23.8% across all tiers would be needed to close the current gap without external subsidy.
23.8% increase needed to break even at 2025 costs  ·  Gap widening annually
📋
Summary for Owner Communication
The 10% cost increase over two years is principally explained by labor market inflation (required by Mexican law) and coastal property insurance (driven by hurricane exposure repricing) — two factors no HOA in the region can avoid. Management has demonstrated discipline: payroll came in 12.7% below budget every year and water costs fell 31.8%. The real conversation that owners need to have is about the structural funding gap: fees have not increased since the current schedule was set, while operating costs grew at 5% per year. The unbudgeted Capital Contribution Recovery line in 2025 indicates external parties have been subsidizing the deficit — a situation that requires full disclosure and a long-term fee adjustment plan. The data supports a fee increase conversation grounded in documented, verifiable costs.

Detailed Forensic Findings
6 KEY FINDINGS
01
Payroll: Largest Risk, Best Discipline
Payroll represents 50–51% of all operating expenditure. The organization achieved under-budget performance every year, averaging 12.7% below budget. Payroll grew from $4.17M (2023) to $4.66M (2025) — an 11.5% increase. This growth rate outpaces flat fee revenue, directly widening the coverage gap each year.
3-Year Total: $13,043,337  ·  Avg Budget Savings: 12.7%/yr
02
Other Expenses: The Forensic Flashpoint
"Otros Egresos Ext." presents the most significant forensic concern. In 2023 it reached $345,845 against a $100,000 budget (246% over). In 2024 it surged to $584,922 (485% over). The 3-year cumulative actual of $1,217,013 vs a $300,000 budget demands sub-ledger documentation.
3-Year Excess: $917,013  ·  Avg Overage: 306%
03
Insurance: Rising Cost Trajectory
Insurance premiums rose from $1.09M (2023) to $1.29M (2025), an 18.6% increase. In 2024 the actual cost exceeded budget by 4.2%. The consistent upward trajectory likely reflects escalating coastal and hurricane-risk insurance costs in Los Cabos.
3-Year Growth: +18.6%  ·  2025 vs Budget: 0.9% under
04
Electricity: Budget Overestimation in 2024
Electricity budget in 2024 was set at $432,000 yet actual consumption came in at $269,801 — a 37.5% underrun. By 2025 actual climbed to $342,578 against a still-inflated $453,600 budget. Persistently overestimated electricity budgets create false available headroom.
2024 Budget Gap: $162,199  ·  3-Year Range: $270K–$369K
05
Maintenance Services: Structural Underfunding
General maintenance exceeded budget all three years: $81,194 vs $29,900 (2023, 171.5% over), $94,718 vs $78,000 (2024), and $123,278 vs $96,000 (2025). Actual spend grew 51.8% from 2023 to 2025, far outpacing the budget increase.
2023 Overage: 171.5%  ·  3-Year Growth: +51.8%
06
Staff Meals: Consistent 39% Growth
Staff meals grew from $232,690 (2023) to $323,733 (2025), a 39.1% increase over three years. The upward trend parallels payroll growth, suggesting this benefit scales proportionally with headcount. Management should confirm whether the benefit has contractual or regulatory basis.
3-Year Growth: +39.1%  ·  2025 Budget Adherence: 6.7% under
